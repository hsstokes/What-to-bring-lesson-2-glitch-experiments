IELS Level 5 · Photoshop Rotation · Pre-Session

✦ What to Bring

Prepare before you arrive — three things, same for everyone
📷 Media 📊 Business ✏️ Design ⏱ 20 min prep
📌 This applies to everyone — Media, Business, and Design

Bring work connected to your own subject area. If it is physical, scan or photograph it flat before you arrive. Save files to USB, Google Drive, or OneDrive — not email.

The Three Things

1
Scanned Work from Class or Homework
Bring something you have already made — from class, a homework task, or your own practice. It can be a sketch, a finished piece, a layout, a photograph, or a page of notes. Physical work must be scanned or photographed flat. Bring at least two or three files so you have options.
e.g. a sketchbook page, a printed layout, a photograph you took, a mood board, a collage, a piece of hand-drawn type
2
A Handwritten Quote or Statement
Choose a quote, artist statement, or short piece of writing by someone you are researching — not your own words. Write it out by hand on plain paper, then scan or photograph it. It does not need to be neat. The handwriting becomes raw material for the glitch text exercise in the session.
e.g. a quote from an artist you are studying, a line from a designer's manifesto, a sentence from a photographer's statement, a famous phrase connected to your subject
3
Images to Expand Your Research
Bring 2–3 images of the person or work you are researching — the artist, designer, or thinker whose quote you chose. These will be used to develop your research pages in the session. Save as JPEG files, clearly named.
e.g. a portrait of the artist, photographs of their work, images from an exhibition, a scanned page from a book about them

If Your Work is Physical — How to Scan

1
Use the library scanner
Scan at 300dpi minimum. 600dpi if the work has fine detail. Save as JPEG or TIFF.
2
Or photograph flat
Lay the work on a table. Photograph directly above in good daylight. Avoid shadows. Your phone camera is fine.
3
Save somewhere accessible
Google Drive, OneDrive, or a USB stick. Not just on your phone — you need to open files in Photoshop.
4
Bring at least 3
More is better. Having options means you are never stuck at the start of the session.
Tip: Name your files clearly — myname-sketchbook.jpg, myname-quote.jpg, myname-artist-ref1.jpg — so you can find them quickly when the session starts.
